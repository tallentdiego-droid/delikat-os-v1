# M6-002 --- Standard Recipe Structure and Product Preparation

## Recipe Components

Every standardized recipe documents: - Recipe identification. - Yield. -
Ingredient list. - Quantity of each ingredient. - Measurement unit. -
Sequential preparation procedure. - Portioning method. - Storage method.

## Prep Recipe Standards

Prep recipes may require: - Advance thawing. - Ingredient preparation. -
Long cooking processes. - Cooling before storage. - Portioning into
standardized units. - Product labeling. - Delivery to warehouse after
completion.

## Kitchen Recipe Standards

Kitchen recipes use prepared products generated by PREP when specified.
Kitchen procedures include: - Product retrieval. - Heating or final
cooking. - Assembly. - Plating. - Presentation verification before
dispatch.

## Portioning

Products requiring storage are portioned using the quantities defined in
the recipe before being stored.

## Storage

Prepared products are: - Cooled according to the documented procedure. -
Packaged using the specified container. - Labeled. - Delivered to
warehouse after packaging.

## Presentation

Finished dishes are compared against the approved presentation reference
before dispatch.
