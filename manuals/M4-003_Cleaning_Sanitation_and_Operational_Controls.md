# M4-003 --- Cleaning, Sanitation and Operational Controls

## Local Cleaning

-   Perform daily cleaning of the entire restaurant.
-   Clean before opening and after closing.
-   Maintain cleanliness during operation.

## General Cleaning Control

-   Complete the general cleaning control according to the assigned
    schedule.
-   The responsible employee performs the task.
-   The supervisor verifies completion.

## Bathroom Cleaning

-   Bathrooms and locker rooms shall be cleaned and disinfected every
    day.
-   Clean after operations or whenever the Customer Service Manager or
    Supervisor considers it necessary.
-   Ventilate bathrooms after cleaning.
-   Rinse and dry floors, toilets, sinks and mirrors after cleaning.
-   Store cleaning chemicals in a safe location away from unauthorized
    personnel.

## Lockers

-   Lockers are for employee use only.
-   Do not store food.
-   Do not store dirty uniforms.
-   Each employee is responsible for keeping their locker clean and
    organized.
-   Management may inspect lockers when inappropriate contents are
    suspected.

## Monthly Bathroom Cleaning Log

Record: - Date - Time - Responsible employee - Trash container -
Mirror - Toilet paper - Soap - Toilet - Sink - Walls - Door - Windows -
Cleaning quality

## Kitchen Cleaning

The Kitchen Manager schedules cleaning tasks for equipment and utensils.
Perform routine cleaning to simplify deep cleaning.

## Bar Cleaning

Maintain scheduled cleaning of all bar equipment, utensils and work
surfaces.

## Service Area Cleaning

Clean service stations, furniture, equipment and guest-contact surfaces
according to schedule.

## Warehouse Cleaning

Maintain warehouse cleanliness and organization. Protect stored products
from contamination.

## Prep Area Cleaning

Maintain cleanliness in the preparation area before, during and after
production.

## Operational Rule

Cleaning records shall be completed by the responsible employee and
verified by the designated supervisor where required.
